# V4 Product Architecture — FROZEN

**Status: FROZEN (owner directive, 2026-07-24). Production development in progress.**

This document is the binding Product & UX source of truth for the entire frontend.
It consolidates the owner-approved design corpus (philosophy blueprint v3 → complete
wireframes → red-team v4 cut → CPO executive review → architecture freeze → Final
Product Architecture Directive) into one in-repo engineering reference. Where any
older document, page, component, or idea conflicts with this one, **this one wins.**

Change control — the architecture may change ONLY on one of four justifications:

1. Real usability testing proves users struggle.
2. Real production data proves an assumption wrong.
3. Engineering constraints make an approach impossible.
4. A demonstrably better solution is discovered that improves the product without
   violating the philosophy.

Personal opinion — including any agent's design taste — is never sufficient.

---

## 1. Governing rules (Final Directive digest)

- **Production mindset.** Nothing temporary. No placeholders, no fake data, no
  "good enough," no intentional tech debt. Everything ships as if it stays for years.
- **Complete frontend reset.** The existing frontend is conceptually deleted. It is
  NOT a design, UX, navigation, or component reference. It survives only as a
  temporary bridge shell connected to the backend until the new product replaces it.
  Nothing from it survives automatically; everything must earn its place under this
  architecture. Never ask "how do I fit this into the existing UI."
- **The backend is untouchable and finished** for presentation purposes: scoring,
  confidence, verdicts, gates, kill criteria, evidence, providers, watchlist
  re-checks. The V2 engine blueprint (docs/PRODUCT_INTELLIGENCE_V2_BLUEPRINT.md)
  remains fully in force on the engine side. V4 is the experience over that engine.
- **Phase discipline.** Phase 1 = Stream, Hunt, Brief, Pull — nothing else. Compare,
  Desk, Record (full), Calibration, and advanced workflows begin only after Phase 1
  is complete, production-ready, and validated against its own success metrics.
- **The final test for every decision:** does this make founders more capable of
  making high-quality business decisions with less cognitive effort and more
  confidence? If no — it does not belong in the product.

## 2. The product in one paragraph

One surface: a stream where a partner speaks first and an input waits. A hunch
becomes a visible hunt, then a Brief that says — in plain words — the answer, the
why, and the one move to make, with the balanced case and the reversal conditions
one scroll below and every claim tappable for grounded interrogation. One
recommended action commits the user with pre-agreed success metrics. Positions live
in a strip that grows into a desk only when the portfolio does; Compare appears when
there are two things to compare; Calibration appears when there is truth to report.
Complexity is earned, vocabulary is taught by subtitles that retire themselves,
navigation is contextual, and every screen must feed the sentence:
**"I know exactly what I'm doing next — and I know what would change my mind."**

## 3. Core principles (all binding)

- The customer wants a decision, not data. The product IS the answer; everything
  else exists to make it believable, falsifiable, and actionable — in that order.
- Answer before evidence, at every depth, on every screen.
- Positions, not opinions: every verdict ships with its reversal conditions
  (real kill criteria + live values), re-checked weekly.
- Confidence is weakest-link, expressed in the partner's own sentence, never as an
  unexplained percentage.
- One user-facing provenance language, three plain words: **measured / my judgment /
  couldn't verify.** (The internal five-level taxonomy stays internal.)
- Emotion is revealed from real data, never generated by the frame. The frame is a
  calm senior analyst; the market provides the drama. No hype, no fake urgency, no
  gamification tokens, no celebration effects.
- Information tiers (universal test — a field must increase confidence, explain the
  verdict, reduce uncertainty, change the decision, or say what to do next):
  - **T1 said unprompted:** verdict-as-words, the why sentence, conviction phrase +
    weakest link, gate caps, top-3 drivers, primary risk.
  - **T2 one scroll:** balanced case detail, reversal conditions + watch state,
    validation plan/budget/success metrics, the window (lifecycle + divergence).
  - **T3 on request:** the full evidence record — tables, charts, quotes, suppliers,
    per-dimension breakdowns.
  - **T4 hidden:** machine metadata, taxonomies, the parallel V2 verdict (voiced
    only on disagreement, labeled), empty fields (render nothing — never a
    placeholder), internal/roadmap language (never anywhere).
- **The score (0–100) does not appear on the Brief.** Its only surface is Compare
  (common scale over the user's own candidates).
- Plain-language first: every term of art carries a subtitle on first encounters
  ("Conviction — how sure I am") that retires itself after a few exposures. No
  tours, no tooltip storms, no onboarding checklists.
- No hover-dependent information anywhere. Mobile-first; desktop widens the measure
  and opens evidence beside the narrative — more room, never more concepts.
- Density gradient: sparse at the answer, moderate through the case, dense inside
  the record. Never inverted.

## 4. The application map & unlock ladder

Zero structural navigation on day one. The product is one surface — the Stream —
with Brief / interrogation / record stacking over it (max sheet depth 2, one back
gesture). Surfaces unlock with the user's own portfolio:

| State | The product is |
| --- | --- |
| First run | One screen, one input, three example hunches. No tabs, no vocabulary. |
| First verdict | The Brief, plain-language mode, one recommended action. |
| First Pull | First contextual fit question; positions strip appears in the stream. |
| 2+ positions | Compare unlocks (offered by the partner in-stream, not as chrome). |
| 3+ positions | Desk unlocks as a view. |
| First tripped condition | The living-position lesson happens in reality. |
| ~10 closed outcomes | Calibration appears. Until then it does not exist anywhere. |

Fit questions are asked one at a time, contextually, at the moment a verdict needs a
personal read — never as an onboarding interview. The fit layer adds a **separate,
labeled** personal sentence; the market verdict is immutable by personalization.
Adaptive behavioral memory (inferred preference learning) is **removed** — it
contradicts the processing-fluency principle. Only explicit, stated fit exists.

## 5. Phase 1 screens (normative anatomy)

### S-Stream (home)
Partner speaks first: what moved since last visit (real re-check/alert output with
the partner's read attached), pending runs, then the input ("Tell me what you're
thinking of building" + three example hunches). Quiet weeks are one quiet line.
Nothing here is ever about the user's "usage" — only about markets and positions.

### S-Hunt (analysis in progress)
Real evidence arriving as it actually arrives — real events only, max ~4 visible
lines, "you can leave" affordance; backgroundable, the reveal waits in the stream.
A batch that completes together is shown completing together. **Never simulated
ticks, never fake incremental progress.**

### S-Brief (the decision artifact)
- **First viewport — exactly three things:** the answer (verdict in plain words,
  the only large element), the why sentence (strongest fact; a gate cap is named as
  a gate), and the one recommended move. Conviction is folded into the answer's own
  language ("I'm fairly sure of this one" / "hold this one loosely").
- **One scroll below:** "The case" — one balanced block, 3 for / 2 against, words
  left, one number right, every claim a tap-to-interrogate handle; then the window
  (in words; the divergence shape only when the data genuinely has it); then the
  reversal conditions with today's live values + watch state; then freshness stamp.
- **Freshness stamp (CPO amendment, mandatory):** "Researched <date> · conditions
  re-checked weekly · the full picture may have moved since."
- Reveal lands as **one beat** (verdict word, then the page; under a second;
  instant under reduced-motion).
- Insufficient evidence is a first-class verdict: "I can't call this one" + which
  channels came up empty + what would make it callable.

### S-Pull (commitment)
One **recommended primary action** (the verb the partner recommends), alternatives
behind "or…". Validate flow shows the real plan: steps, real budget, and
**pre-agreed success metrics** ("I'll hold you to these numbers") — the
anti-sunk-cost device. Watch confirms exactly what is being guarded. Kill archives
with an optional reason; the partner offers redirection only when real adjacent
signals exist. The chosen state persists on the position and drives the stream.

### Interrogation (within Phase 1, tap-driven only)
Tap any claim → a sheet answering about exactly that claim, grounded strictly in
this analysis's evidence record, each number carrying its source. Phase 1 ships
this as **templated grounded lookups (no free-text LLM chat)**. The free-text
conversational layer is a later phase, gated on a grounding/voice eval suite and on
real tap-engagement data. Suggested taps appear after the verdict only, max three.

## 6. The partner's voice (spec — copy is the product)

Senior, terse, first person ("My call…", "I'd test margin first"). Numbers in every
load-bearing sentence. Confidence stated like a human. Says "I don't know" cleanly
and "I was wrong" when a re-check flips a call. Never flatters, never hypes, never
hedges into mush. Professional, not a mascot — no avatar, no moods, no catchphrases.
Directive framing for the recommended action ("If it were my money: …") pending the
pre-launch legal/liability review of voice directiveness (CPO amendment).

## 7. Never do (complete, binding)

Never: two answers to one question at once (a second verdict/score appears only on
labeled disagreement) · a control that does nothing · placeholders or roadmap
language · naked numbers without a claim · evidence before the answer · precision
beyond evidence · hidden uncertainty · a screen ending in data instead of an action
or the answer restated · repeated facts on one page · transparency disclosures
behind hover, or shown twice · aggregate product statistics masquerading as customer
information · hype the evidence doesn't contain · manufactured urgency/scarcity ·
simulated progress or fake delays · gamification points/streaks/badges · celebration
effects · making a Skip feel like a punishment (it is a save) · letting the fit
layer touch the market verdict · answering beyond the evidence record.

## 8. Phase plan & validation gates (CPO amendments, binding)

Each phase defines its success metrics and stop conditions BEFORE it is built —
the product's own discipline applied to itself.

- **Phase 1:** Stream, Hunt, Brief, Pull (+ tap-interrogation as grounded lookups).
  Gate metrics defined in docs/RD_V4_PHASE1.md.
- **Phase 2+ (only after Phase 1 validates):** Compare → Desk → full Record
  chapters → free-text conversational layer (after eval suite + tap-engagement
  evidence) → Calibration (after real closed outcomes) → shared one-pager as the
  growth artifact.
