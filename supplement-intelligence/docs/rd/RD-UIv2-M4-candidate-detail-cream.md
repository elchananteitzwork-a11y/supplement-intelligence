Owner-directed milestone (2026-07-22/23). Supersedes the archived dark "night world" prototype per `docs/DESIGN_LANGUAGE_PLAN.md` §5 item 2 (owner-confirmed) and implements the reframe from `docs/UX_EXPERIENCE_PLAN.md` §2 row 6: the front of Candidate Detail becomes one calm verdict card (real WebGL rotor as centerpiece) + a `Sources` toggle carrying the full 14-section report, instead of 14 sections stacked by default. - **`components/memo/MemoDisplay.tsx`** — reused 100% unmodified. All 14 sections (`CurrentSignal` through `LaunchStrategy`, confirmed exhaustively by the architecture audit) stay exactly as they are today, content and logic untouched — only newly wrapped in a collapsed-by-default toggle.
- **The `TechnicalDetailToggle` idiom** (`components/memo/CurrentSignal.tsx:157-167`, "Show … →" pattern, `useState(expanded)`) — reused as the pattern for the new Sources toggle, not a new interaction language. - **AppShell/SideNav (`variant="pi"`)** — already wired on `/memo/[id]` (`app/memo/[id]/page.tsx:38`), confirmed by the architecture audit. No shell change needed, unlike Home's milestone.
- **Material/lighting decisions (real, owner-approved this session, via isolated research harness — never touched production files during exploration):**
  - Reflection: drei `<Environment>` fed by procedural `<Lightformer>` primitives (`MeshBasicMaterial` geometry only, no textures, `frames={1}` one-time bake) — confirmed zero network dependency (verified via real `browser_network_requests` checks), unlike drei's stock `preset`/`useMatcapTexture` helpers, which DO fetch from `cdn.jsdelivr.net` and were rejected for exactly that reason.
  - Contact shadow: drei `<ContactShadows>`, confirmed present in the installed `@react-three/drei@9.122.0`, confirmed built entirely from `WebGLRenderTarget`/`PlaneGeometry`/`MeshDepthMaterial` (no texture/network dependency). Owner-chosen strength: "pronounced" (opacity 0.62, blur 2.0) over "subtle" (0.4/3.4) — the subtle tuning was visually too faint to register as intentional in live review.
  - Explicitly rejected, do not implement: rim/backlight (owner chose reflection-alone over the combo), per-blade color variation (owner didn't select it; also directly reverses a locked comment in `CandidateCoreRotor.tsx` — "locked brand gold — never changes with data" — deferred as its own future decision, not silently applied).

## 4. Risks

- **DOM-overlay rewrite is real, non-trivial work** — not a color-token find/replace, a genuine new layout matching the approved mockup exactly, including the 5-second-comprehension revision (verdict-word dominance, de-emphasized signal grid, neutral kill-line). Testing plan below verifies against the approved mockup screenshot-for-screenshot where practical.
- **Contact shadow's camera-framing constraint** (disclosed by research): the rotor's bounding circle nearly touches the viewport edge at the current `fov`/camera distance, so the shadow is confined to a thin band at the bottom, not a full "puddle." This is a known, accepted tradeoff from live review, not a defect to silently fix.
- **`sourcesCount` is a new real field** — low risk (one-line read of an already-computed value), but needs a real test asserting it matches `evidenceBreadth.contributingProviders.length` exactly, not a re-derived approximation.
- **Blade-click-to-section deep-linking stays unresolved** (pre-existing, disclosed open question in `coreDataAdapter.ts`'s own comments, predates this milestone) — the new signal grid is NOT wired to scroll-to-section on click in this milestone; clicking `Sources` reveals the whole report at once. A future milestone could add per-blade deep-linking; out of scope here (see Non-Goals).
- **Verdict-color-mapping question stays open** — the owner asked in the mockup-review pass whether "Validate Further"'s blue should become amber/traffic-light; explicitly deferred as a cross-screen decision (would also affect Home's already-shipped chips and Compare's). Not resolved by this milestone.
- **Reduced-motion coverage for the new material additions** — confirmed via the research harness that `ContactShadows` needs explicit `frames` capping under `reduceMotion`, already planned into the Canvas.tsx change above; needs a real regression test, not just visual inspection, since a silent per-frame cost under "frozen" motion would be a real accessibility/performance regression.
