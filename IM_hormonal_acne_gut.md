## 1. Executive Summary

A daily supplement targeting adult women with hormonal (chin/jaw) acne by addressing the clinically documented gut-skin axis — the internal inflammatory pathway that topical treatments cannot reach. Positioned as the only supplement that explicitly connects gut dysbiosis to hormonal acne as its primary consumer message. The category has been validated by CLEARSTEM ($50M, 95% DTC, self-funded since 2018) — a skincare-first brand whose MINDBODYSKIN supplement sells as a secondary product. The gap: no supplement-first brand has made the gut-skin mechanism its primary claim. Clinical foundation is strong: a 2024 RCT showed oral probiotics improved acne severity in 50% of patients; a 12-week RCT showed L. rhamnosus GG reduced skin inflammatory cytokines IL-6 and TNF-α. The category intersects two of the fastest-growing TikTok verticals: skincare (129B #acne views) and gut health. Formula must include DIM + BioPerine (the absorption fix most competitors skip), zinc bisglycinate, spearmint extract, and acne-specific probiotic strains. Entry narrative: "Your hormonal acne isn't a skin problem. It starts in your gut."

**Is this worth building? → YES**

## 7. Market Gaps (10)

1. **No brand owns the gut → hormonal acne mechanism** — the science is published, the positioning is unclaimed
2. **DIM without BioPerine is almost unabsorbed** — the #1 formulation failure across all DIM supplements; fixing this alone creates a meaningfully superior product
3. **Post-birth-control acne segment is unaddressed** — millions of women experience a hormone crash and severe acne after stopping birth control; no supplement is specifically positioned for this moment
4. **Pregnancy-safe hormonal acne formula** — Spiro is contraindicated in pregnancy; no natural supplement brands own this positioning despite it being the fastest-growing demand segment
5. **90-day expectation education gap** — customers abandon at week 2 on a problem that takes 8–12 weeks to resolve; no brand builds an educational protocol into their subscription
6. **Clean 4-ingredient formula** — market is full of 10–20 ingredient kitchen-sink products; a simple, auditable formula stands out
7. **Biotin-free and iodine-free positioning** — both are common acne triggers that appear in many "clear skin" supplements; being explicitly free of both is a meaningful safety signal
8. **Gut-skin test + supplement bundle** — no one has paired a gut microbiome test with a targeted acne supplement protocol
9. **The "nothing worked topically" audience** — women who have exhausted prescription and OTC topicals are specifically looking for an internal solution; no brand has made this audience their primary target
10. **Men with hormonal acne** — a growing underserved segment; DIM + zinc formula works across genders but all current positioning is female

## 10. Product Recommendation

### Formula
| Ingredient | Dose | Role |
|---|---|---|
| DIM (Brassica extract) | 200mg | Estrogen metabolism — 2-OH pathway shift |
| **BioPerine (black pepper)** | 5mg | **Increases DIM absorption up to 20x — the differentiator** |
| Indole-3-Carbinol (I3C) | 100mg | DIM precursor; potentiates estrogen clearance |
| Zinc bisglycinate | 25mg | Anti-inflammatory, anti-androgenic, sebum regulation |
| Spearmint extract (50:1) | 400mg | Two RCTs confirm free testosterone reduction in women |
| L. rhamnosus GG | 5B CFU | 12-week RCT: improved skin clarity + decreased IL-6/TNF-α |
| L. rhamnosus CECT 30031 | 1B CFU | 2024 RCT: 50% of patients improved ≥1 acne severity category |

### Key Ingredients to AVOID
- Biotin above 300mcg (documented acne trigger; interferes with thyroid testing)
- Iodine (acne trigger at standard supplement doses)
- High-dose vitamin B6 (can trigger acne in some women)
- Kelp / seaweed-derived ingredients (iodine content)
