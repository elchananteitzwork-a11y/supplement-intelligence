## Root cause: why the scoring missed so many

The scoring engine is working correctly given its inputs. The problem is the input: a **best-sellers sweep returns the most established, most competitive products on Amazon.** For new-seller opportunity research, the correct workflow is:

1. Use this scan to identify **what sub-niches sell** (ice cube trays, kitchen towels, accessories for viral drinkware, disposable tableware).
2. Run a **targeted sub-category scan** at a lower BSR range (e.g., node for "Ice Cube Trays" filtered to products ranked 500–5,000 in Kitchen).
3. Look for the combination: BSR 500–3,000 + reviews < 200 + no dominant brand.

Three scoring engine limitations visible in this data:

1. **Brand-blindness** — the engine has no signal for "Ninja", "YETI", "Stanley", "Gorilla Grip". A product with 11,000 reviews from Ninja and one with 11,000 reviews from a generic brand score identically on the review_barrier factor.

2. **Velocity at max for 0.0/mo** — 14 of 20 products showed 0.0 reviews/mo from sparse history data, all scoring 6/6. This inflated scores by ~2–4 points uniformly.

3. **Seasonal 100/100** — all 100 products were flagged seasonal, each losing 2–3 points. The Kitchen BSR max/min threshold of >1.6× is too loose.
